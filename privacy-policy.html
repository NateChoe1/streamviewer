"You" and "the user" refers to you, a person who authorizes their Google account to the streamviewer program.

"This service", "the service", "this program", "the program", and similar derivations refer to the streamviewer program.

"Google" refers to Google LLC.

"Youtube" refers to the video and livestreaming service created by Google.

Part 2: The TOS and privacy policy

This page is hosted with Cloudflare pages, and the backend is hosted with Cloudflare workers. Cloudflare may collect certain information about your browser, including your IP address, user agent, and other browser metadata. I am not liable for the data that Cloudflare collects. Cloudflare's privacy policy can be viewed at https://www.cloudflare.com/privacypolicy/.

Youtube wants me to add a link to their terms of service, so here it is: https://www.youtube.com/t/terms

This service utilizes the Youtube APIs, which are subject to Google's privacy policy, located at https://www.google.com/policies/privacy.

Despite the fact that Youtube and Google hire thousands of the smartest engineers on the planet, it's not possible, or at least very difficult, to write a program to view a Youtube live chat without logging in to a Google account. When you log in to a Google account, Google generates an "oauth2 token", which programs can use to access certain Google services. For some reason, you need an oauth2 token to get a stream chat ID. This requires you to log in to the streamviewer service. The oauth2 token never leaves your computer, and is never stored on your computer.

This also means that I can't revoke the oauth2 token that was generated, since I never get it in the first place. This is a problem, as Youtube requires that I provide a way to revoke oauth2 tokens. Oauth2 tokens can be revoked through the Google security settings page at https://security.google.com/settings/security/permissions. This is the official way to revoke tokens, because I literally cannot do anything on my end.

You can contact the developer at nate@natechoe.dev.

You are allowed to exploit/hack/break streamviewer, under the conditions that you don't collect any user data, that you don't cause unnecessary damage beyond proving that streamviewer is exploitable, and that you responsibly disclose any vulnerabilities you find to nate@natechoe.dev.

End TOS and privacy policy
